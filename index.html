Projects

Digital Windex (ongoing)

Digital Windex is an innovative project that I conceptualized and pitched, earning selection among a competitive pool of student ideas. Along with my talented team, we’re developing an all-in-one Windows maintenance tool designed to automate system diagnostics, repair, and optimization tasks. Built in Visual Studio 2022 using C#, .NET, and WPF, our solution features a clean, jargon-free, and modular design tailored for non-technical users. By consolidating powerful Windows utilities—such as DISM, Windows Memory Diagnostics, and Windows Update—into a single intuitive application, Digital Windex empowers users to effortlessly maintain their systems at peak performance.

Predictive Analysis on Movie Attendance

In a group project, I explored movie-going behaviors to help optimize strategies for the film industry. We integrated primary survey data from Google Forms with secondary data from Statista and analyzed demographic factors, genre preferences, and movie viewing habits using Python. Our approach combined statistical methods—such as descriptive statistics, chi-square tests, and correlation analysis—with predictive modeling techniques including decision trees, logistic regression, and artificial neural networks (ANN). This analysis provided actionable insights into consumer behavior and movie attendance trends.

Tools Used: Python, Pandas, NumPy, Matplotlib, Seaborn, Scikit-learn, Graphviz, TensorFlow (Keras API), Google Forms, and Statista.
